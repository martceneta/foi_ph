Freedom of Information

The Freedom of Information (FOI) Program is the Government’s response to the call for transparency and full public disclosure of information. FOI is a government mechanism, which allows Filipino citizens to request any information about government transactions and operations, provided that it shall not put into jeopardy – privacy and matters of national security.

Proactive

Transparency Seal

Government information is a pearl, meant to be shared with the public in order to maximize its inherent value. The Transparency Seal, depicted by a pearl shining out of an open shell, is a symbol of a policy shift towards openness in access to government information.

Proactive

Open Data Philippines

aims to institutionalize good governance by making government data available to the public. This involves collating datasets from different government agencies, cleaning them for better understandability, and uploading them to a website in open formats.

The FOI Program

It has been five (5) years since President Rodrigo Roa Duterte issued Executive No. 2 s. 2016 which allowed the operationalization of the people's right to Information in the Executive branch. The PCOO was thereafter designated as the lead agency in the implementation of the FOI Program. The FOI-PMO was subsequently established under the PCOO to monitor compliance and lead the FOI Program, including the Electronic FOI in the Executive branch. Since then, it has continuously expanded its efforts in fostering a nation of truly engaged and well-informed citizenry by promoting access to information through standard paper-based and electronic platforms.
